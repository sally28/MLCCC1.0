Choose any one of the following options to register for classes:

  • On line:Register online here. Create an account if you are new. Select classes. Pay online.

  • By mail: Print registration form, fill out, sign, and mail the completed form with your payment to MLCCC's mailing address.

  • In person: Go to our Weekend School during school hours or go to our Summer Camp during camp hours and give us the registration and payment. Please check our school calendar posted on our homepage for days and hours our school is in session, and our Summer Camp webpage for camp’s days and hours.

Students can withdraw from a class at any time. To withdraw from a class, please send an email to info@mlccc.org and state the student's name, class name, time of the class, and effective date of the withdrawal. You can also go to the school administrator's desk to complete and hand in a withdrawal form.

First class is a free trial:
  • If decide to register after the trial, then must register for the entire school year and pay the registration fee
  • If decide not to register after the trial, then you will get full refund of tuition payment, including registration fee if paid any.
Withdrawal after the second class:
  • Registration fees are non-refundable.
  • Unused portion of tuition minus a handling fee of $10 per student will be refunded.
Long term leave
  • For continuous leave of 3 weeks or more, such as for travel, notify school 1 month in advance for full refund of missed classes.

The MLCCC Weekend School holds several academic contests each year to promote students' interest in Chinese language learning by challenging them to realize their full Chinese language potential.

Song Li Cup Chinese Essay/Story Contest (李松杯中文写作比赛)
Sponsored by Song Li China Culture Foundation
Format: Essay or picture story.
Groups: Students are grouped by age and CHL or CSL.
Must include participant’s name, class, teacher’s name, parent’s or guardian’s contact email on each essay.
All essays will be published in MLCCC’s Official WeChat Account.
Prizes:Participation awards and souvenirs for all and prizes for top performers.
View past essays here.
Chinese Speech Contest
Format:Recite from memory in front of a panel of judges.
Groups:Students are grouped by grade level and CHL or CSL. Points are deducted for speeches that are too short or too long. Prizes: Participation awards and prizes for top performers.
View past contest videos here.
Chinese Character Recognition Contest
Format:This is similar to a vocabulary test. A contestant is presented with Chinese words or characters by a judge, and needs to instantly recall the pronunciation of the word or character and sometimes also its meaning. A contestant is not competing against another student. Simply, the more characters a contestant recognizes, the higher his/her score is.
Eligibility:
  • CHL Grade 1 and up
  • CSL Level 1 and up
Rules:
  • Characters are taken from each grade level’s textbook vocabulary.
  • Both current grade level’s vocabulary and prior grade levels’ vocabulary are tested.
  • Depending on grade level and whether CHL or CSL, each contestant is usually presented with at least 100, sometimes 150 characters.
  • CHL students just need to pronounce the characters.
  • CSL students need to also give the meaning of the characters.
Prizes:
Participation awards and prizes for top performers. Word Lists
  • CHL MLP Word List
  • CHL MZHY Word List
  • CHL AP Word List
  • CSL Word List

MLCCC Weekend School - Persistency Award for Chinese Learning - Guidelines

The Main Line Chinese Culture Center has established a "Persistency Award for Chinese Learning" in our Chinese Weekend School in order to award those of our students who have persistently spent years of personal time learning Chinese by attending our classes and doing the homework. We also hope that this award will encourage all our Weekend School students to demonstrate a persistent effort and take pride in learning Chinese and attending Chinese Weekend School.
The guidelines for the Persistency Award for Chinese Learning are as follows:

  1. A student who has completed the below number of years/terms of our 2-hour Chinese Heritage Language (CHL) course or Chinese as a Second Language (CSL) course in MLCCC Weekend School will be awarded a Persistency Award Certificate and the corresponding tuition credit at the end of the eligible school year.
    • 3 years (or 9 terms) of CHL or CSL classes - certificate and $100 tuition credit (cumulative)
    • 6 years (or 18 terms) of CHL or CSL classes - certificate and $250 tuition credit (cumulative)
    • 10 years (or 30 terms) of CHL or CSL classes - certificate and $500 tuition credit (cumulative)
    (CHL classes are the Chinese language classes that most of our students are enrolled in.) The above award levels are cumulative -- any student who qualifies for the 6-year award who has already received the 3-year award will receive the difference between the two award levels (i.e. $150 tuition credit).
    The tuition credit is only transferrable within the same family (such as to a younger sibling).
  2. The qualifying years/terms do not need to be consecutive, as long as the total number of years/terms enrolled in MLCCC's CHL or CSL classes reaches the above levels. For example, students who have left the school and come back can still count the time they have accrued before leaving toward this award.
  3. Accumulations from different years are acceptable. For example, since our school year consists of 3 terms, as long as you accumulate 9 terms (3 terms per year x 3 years) of CHL or CSL class time, then you qualify for the $100 award level.
  4. The qualifying years/terms will be determined according to MLCCC Weekend School's registration records.
  5. The award is focused on students' persistence, regardless of their grades and/or performance.
  6. The award is focused on learning the Chinese language. Therefore the period that a student registers for only culture classes such as Drawing, Karate, etc. will not count toward the award.
  7. Qualified students need to apply for the award and only currently registered Weekend School students are eligible to apply.
  8. All teachers' and administrators' children are qualified to receive the certificate but not the monetary award.

We congratulate our students who qualify for any of the above mentioned award levels, and encourage more students to reach the highest-level possible!

MLCCC PTO Committee

MLCCC PTO is made up of parent volunteers to serve our students, teachers and parents.
Our mission is to enhance the cultural experience and help build a community for MLCCC families from all different backgrounds. In addition to assisting school administrators with events, PTO will host additional activities to enrich our students’ learning environment. Together with the excellent academic programs by our dedicated teaching staff, these activities provide a holistic approach to learning both the Chinese language and its rich and extensive culture.
As the old saying goes: It takes a village to raise a child! Parent participation is essential to the success of our PTO. We truly appreciate the talents and enthusiasm of any parent volunteer. We welcome you to participate or help in any way you prefer. Please feel free to ask questions and/or contribute ideas during school hours or email to PTO.

MLCCC Clubs

MLCCC strives to provide safe and comfortable spaces for our parents to enjoy while their children are learning. All clubs are free, while donation is appreciated to help with the rental expense.

Basketball Club: welcomes parents from around the community. Our indoor court ensures that the fun is all year round!

Fitness Club: from aerobics to Zumba, it welcomes young and old for a sweating fun time together.

Karaoke, Games, and Cards Club: Whether you enjoy dueling quietly over a chess board, or singing your hearts out in front of a microphone, this club has something for everyone!

Photography and Video Club: learn to take awesome photos of your children! The Club has been a key part in audio, video, and photographic support of schoolwide events throughout the year. Thank you!

Story Club: is the perfect place for preschoolers to be while waiting for their older siblings to finish class. Listen to a story, do a craft, learn some Chinese.

Taiji Club: led by the nationally certified Taiji instructor Ms. Shao, there is no reason not to join. It's fun and healthy for young and old, body and mind.

Tea Club: Come and sip a cup of tea, take a load off, enjoy a good conversation. All supplies are contributed by club members.

Yoga Club: is taught by a registered Power Yoga teacher (RYT-200) and member of the Power Yoga Alliance, Ms. Esther Estes who is also a former physician. You get sore but it is so good for you.

School Store

The purpose of the School Store is to provide healthy snacks and drinks to our students. It is run by our PTO and other parent volunteers. It opens every Sunday during our school hours. Our students love the School Store; they enjoy shopping/purchasing their favorite items with their hard-earned school dollars. Items are provided by our PTO and we also welcome donations from parents. Most wanted items include:

  1. Healthy snacks
  2. Organic milk
  3. Small size Chinese culture related artifacts
