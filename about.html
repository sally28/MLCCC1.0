MLCCC History

Established in 2003, the Main Line Chinese Culture Center, Inc (MLCCC or Ming Lang) is a nonprofit-501c3, nonpartisan, nonpolitical organization that serves as a catalyst to increase understanding between American and Chinese cultures. The MLCCC's mission is to provide compelling insights of China's culture to interested families within the greater Philadelphia area in order to support their educational needs and career aspirations. The vision for the group is to grow its operations such that its diversified sources of funding will enable the organization to secure its own site facility, full-time staff, and financial self-sufficiency that will lead to the most effective Chinese cultural education instruction within the state of Pennsylvania.

The MLCCC provides a window to Chinese culture by offering basic language instruction (Mandarin) as well as classes in traditional Chinese dance and other art forms (Chinese painting, self-defense, etc.). Through weekend and summer classes taught at rented facilities, MLCCC instructors leverage a traditional Asian teaching style that emphasizes strong study skills and discipline. Since its inception in 2003, the MLCCC has grown from an informal group of thirty students to an enrollment of over three hundred students by the end of 2008. Currently, three part-time staff, 16 instructors, and a very considerate group of parent volunteers serve a PreK-12th grade student population of which 87% are of Chinese-American descent. Satisfied parents have confirmed the value of the program model as the MLCCC's growth has been driven primarily by word of mouth alone. Finally, global CEOs like Ethnic Technologies' Zack Wilhoit have commented, "The Main Line Chinese Culture Center reaches out to so many children and their families with valuable insights on Chinese-American culture and the significant implications for our future relationships with China".

MLCCC PTO Committee

MLCCC PTO is made up of parent volunteers to serve our students, teachers and parents.
Our mission is to enhance the cultural experience and help build a community for MLCCC families from all different backgrounds. In addition to assisting school administrators with events, PTO will host additional activities to enrich our students’ learning environment. Together with the excellent academic programs by our dedicated teaching staff, these activities provide a holistic approach to learning both the Chinese language and its rich and extensive culture.
As the old saying goes: It takes a village to raise a child! Parent participation is essential to the success of our PTO. We truly appreciate the talents and enthusiasm of any parent volunteer. We welcome you to participate or help in any way you prefer. Please feel free to ask questions and/or contribute ideas during school hours or email to PTO.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)
Course FAQs for Sunday Classes
Transportation
  • Where is the closest parking lot?

    • Complementary parking is available at the school. Although not posted, we request that visitors do not double-park at the lot.

Skills/Class Structure
  • How do I know which level my child should take?

    • The MLCCC does provide an informal interview to test a student's language ability. Please contact Principal Li Xa (xlihuangpu@hotmail.com ) to schedule such an interview. After your child attends the preliminary class, a decision can be made to stay or move to another class.

  • If my child misses class due to illness, etc., can they receive homework to catch up?

    • Yes, parents can request homework updates from the instructors.

  • Is the language instructor a native Chinese speaker?

    • Yes, all instructors are native Chinese speakers. However for certain MLCCC enrichment courses (SAT prep, etc.), not all instructors are native Chinese speakers.

  • How often do classes meet?

    • Classes are held each Sunday at 1:00pm. Class terms are impacted by holidays, etc. which are communicated well in advance

  • Can I receive college credit for a class?

    • Not at this time.

  • What are the age spans that are served by the MLCCC?

    • The weekend program at Malvern supports mostly PreK-12 youth, however adults participate in cultural enrichment courses (traditional dance, choir, etc.). In addition, the MLCCC is fielding inquires regarding cultural programs for adults interested in business and cultural topics. For more information, please email us at info@mlccc.org.

Payment
  • Can I receive a refund if I decide to quit class in the middle of the term?

    • If a parent needs to withdraw his/her child, the MLCCC will refund a pro-rated amount for remaining classes based upon the terms of the school policy.

  • If I already have the text book that the school uses, do I need to purchase it again as part of the fee structure?

    • No, if the student already has the current version of the class textbook, he/she does not need to purchase it again. Note, the MLCCC provides its books at cost and does not make a profit on such transactions.

  • How can I pay for my child's class?

    • Pre-payment is required to be considered fully registered for a class. Payment can be made by check or cash. Please make check payable to the Main Line Chinese Culture Center Inc. and mail it to MLCCC, P. O. Box 745, Bryn Mawr, PA 19010 or bring to the weekend school.
