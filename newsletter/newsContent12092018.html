PTO sponsored the "Wall of Gratitude" was a great success! We literally have a wall of gratitude messages from so many students. Some of them are touching, some of them are funny, some of our creative, yet some of them are imaginative. We are indeed grateful to have so many grateful children.

1. PTO’s First “Book Exchange & Sale” Begins This Week in Admin Hallway, Runs for Two Weeks.

1) All transactions are done through “tickets”.  

 

2) To buy books: first buy tickets beforehand, at $1 = 4 tickets.

 

3) To sell books:

- You decide on how many books to sell and how many tickets to charge for each book.

(PTO's recommendation is to sell fewer than 5 books and each book for no more than 25 tickets.)

- Label each book clearly with # of tickets needed to buy it.  

- Lay out a small box next to your books, so people can place tickets into the box (honor system) to buy your books.

- Take your books and box home every Sunday.

 

4) Tickets not used or left over at the end can be exchanged back into money.

 

2. Weekend School’s Math Teachers Invited to Coach Radnor School District’s MATHCOUNTS Team and Math Club

Mr. Tom Le, our MATHCOUNTS teacher, has been asked by Radnor Middle School to be its MATHCOUNTS team’s official coach. He will be leading Radnor students as they compete in the MATHCOUNTS Delaware County Chapter Competition in February 2019.

 

Dr. Robert Hsu, our Math Enrichment teacher, has been asked to be Radnor Elementary School’s Math Club teacher.

 

Congratulations to both!

 

3. Interest in 24 Game?

We wanted to ask if students are still interested in participating in the mathematical 24 Game (applying addition, subtraction, multiplication, division to 4 numbers to get 24), which has traditionally been held in January.  If only a few students are interested, we will not hold it.

Alan Liu takes a break at the Wall of the Gratitude during recess patrol. Please support their work by obeying their safety instructions. Thank you!
