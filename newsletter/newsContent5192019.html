Dear Parents,

 

Time flies. With our Assembly Day this Sunday, this school year has come to an end, and it will end with a BANG! We hope you can join us for a fun day with delicious dishes to share, impressive awards to show our pride, and games to show our competitive spirits.

 

Assembly Day Parent On Duty/周日值班:

We need many hands to setup, cleanup and keep the order, please help if you can, no signup, just show up and line up, thanks in advance!

 

 

We have three BIG events planned for you, Here are the details:

 

Event # 1: MLCCC Potluck Luncheon & Social

Time: 12:00 PM – 1:00PM

Location: GVHS Cafeteria  

Seating: Please SIT by Class with Teacher (if present, recommended).

Activities: Eat, chat, and catch up with teachers and each other

 

The lunch will start at 12:00 noon PROMPTLY, so please arrive a few mins early to get in line and find the recommended tables.

You cook it, you share it, we all enjoy it and appreciate it!

During the potluck lunch, we encourage you to sit with your children’s teacher and other parents in the class. The teacher may use this opportunity to hand out children's homework, graduation certificate, or answer questions you may have.

More importantly, it is a great opportunity for parents to get to know your teacher better. As you may know, most of our teachers have full-time jobs and work hard to be ready to teach every Sunday, they couldn’t do the wonderful jobs they have been doing without the unwavering support of their families. So take this opportunity to meet your teacher, hug them, thank them for another great year, and show your appreciation and gratitude to both teachers and their families.

 

During the luncheon, we kindly ask all parents to observe the following:

-Supervise your children at all times

-Ask your children to take queue in line for food, drinks, and desserts

-Supervise your children not to waste any food or drink

-Tell children not to run around the food/drink set up the table

-Tell children not to run with utensils in hand/mouth

-Please keep conversation down so that others can hear each other

-No children allowed in the front stage area (projector/screen)

-Help with cleanup if you can to reduce custodian hours

-Label your plate/cup to reduce waste

-Please do not litter, use trash bags/cans

 

Most of the potluck dishes are donated by parents, teachers, and PTO. They have worked hard to carefully prepare these dishes to share with everyone, so we ask everybody to be respectful, thankful, and not wasteful. Encourage your children to ask questions about the food, its origins, style, stories behind it, etc. After all, it is part of our traditions and culture. Thank you for your cooperation. Big thank you to our PTO Potluck Team for their careful planning.

 

Event #2” Award Ceremony and Special Recognitions

Time: 1:00PM -2:30PM

Location: Cafeteria

Activities: Award Hand Out, Special Recognitions

Special: MLCCC Spotlight Production, Live Audio, and Video Recording

 

This is the most important part of our Assembly. We are very proud to say that this year our students have performed very well in many areas, especially in school-wide contests such as character recognition, speech, writing, and also taking part on the wall of gratitude. We also have so many TAs, parents, PTO to thank too. We will have many awards to give out, and many people to recognize including two 10-year service teachers. So please stay where you are in the Cafeteria and show your support while enjoying the desserts.

 

Once the ceremony starts, parents and children don't have to stop eating, but we do ask everyone to please observe the following:

-Please stay inside the Cafeteria to cheer for our children,

-Applaud often for our teachers, volunteer parents, TAs,

-Refrain from walking across the room,

-Refrain from conversing with each other loudly,

-Refrain from making distracting noises,

-Keep your children closely supervised and quiet.

 

During the Ceremony, we also ask all the parents to please pay attention to the front stage and show your support by cheering and applauding, especially for our children. Remember we need to show our children that we care about every accomplishment, small or big, that they have achieved during the school year, and that we are all proud of them!  This support will help keep our children interested in learning our language, culture, and heritage.

It takes a community to educate our children, we are a part of it. Thank you.

 

Event #3: MLCCC Annual Sports Day:

Time: 2:30PM – 4:00PM

Location: Baseball Field (next to the Stadium)

Invitation: everyone, young and young at hearts.

Activities: Jump, Run and Tag, here comes the games

 

The last part of our Assembly day is the much anticipated Annual Sports Day. if you have read our newsletter and our WeChat postings, you must have heard of it, and probably are very excited about it.  Our PTO Sports Day Team has worked hard and has planned a dozen carefully chosen games/activities for both children and adults. Compete by yourself, compete in a team, compete as a family, everyone is welcome to join, CHECK OUT THE GAME, FIELD MAP Here.

 

In taking part in MLCCC Sports Day games, We do ask parents to observe the following while having a fun time:

 

-Forecast for Sunday afternoon is going to be very warm (sunny skies, 80’s F)

-Dress properly, wear proper shoes and hydrate as needed (bring your water bottles)

-Know the limit, don’t do what your body can’t handle

-Use safety precautions while engaging in intense competitions

-Apply sun lotion, wear protective gears such as hats, sunglasses, stay in the shade

-Read the Sports Days Liability Disclaimer (Attached), and be responsible for your own safety.

-Show off your MLCCC Spirit wearing MLCCC T's, we will have T-Shirts for sale on site in case you need to buy one.

 -Have fun, be safe, you are responsible for the safety of your family

-We do have a First-Aid Kit and ice on hand


P.S., due to another concurrent event at the high school, the upper parking lot may be full when we arrive at the school, please use side lot or back lot to park. Watch your children while crossing the parking lots.

 

LAST BUT NOT LEAST, we wish everyone a safe and fun summer break, we will see you at our summer camp, on the Wechat or via our newsletters. Travel is a great opportunity for children to practice their language and culture learning, please keep us posted of their adventures throughout the summer.
