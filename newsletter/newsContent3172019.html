Contest Rules:

Structure/process is the same as last year, EXCEPT for:

- MLP G5 words are now 马立平的西游记 words. (Reminder that students still need to know all prior grades' words.)

- Reduced number of CSL Level 4 words and created CSL Level 5+ words.

 

Eligibility: at least CHL Grade 1, or CSL Level 1, or equivalent (students not in a language class who wants to participate needs to be assessed and placed).

 

1. Contestants will be tested based on language track, i.e. CHL (MLP, MZHY, AP) or CSL, and grade level.  

 

1A: CHL MLP of CHL MZHY,

- Most of the words tested will be from their current grade level but some of the words will be from EACH prior grade.

- Read out the words accurately.

 

1B: AP Class students,

- All of the words tested will be from their current grade level.

- Read out the words accurately.

 

1C: CSL,

- All of the words tested will be from their current grade level.

- Read out the words accurately AND translate into English.           

 

2. The contest will be carried out via a PPT (PowerPoint slide show) on a laptop, and the screen automatically advances to the next word after a pre-defined time duration (in seconds). A judge can advance manually if a student finishes a slide faster.

 

3. Partial credit will be given.

 

4. MLCCC Chinese Essay Contest Submissions Begin

The contest is open to CHL grades 2 and up, and all CSL levels. It is completely voluntary and the minimum characters required has been lowered.

 CHL 2nd grade to 3rd grade: A Picture Story of your chosen topic (minimum 100 characters).

CHL 4th grade to 5th grade: A Picture Story or Essay of your chosen topic (minimum 200 characters).

CHL 6th grade and above: Essay of your chosen topic (minimum 300 characters).

CSL level I to II: A Picture Story of your chosen topic (minimum 50 characters).

CSL level III to IV: A Picture Story of your chosen topic (minimum 100 characters).

CSL level V and above: Essay of your chosen topic (minimum 150 characters).

All participants will be awarded 5 school dollars for participation.   Picture stories can be drawn and handwritten.  Essay submissions must be typed. Deadline for submission is 4/14/2019.  Submit to Admin Desk.  Grading will be based on the Pennsylvania Writing Assessment Rubric, which evaluates five areas: Focus, Content, Organization, Style, and Conventions.

Top performers will be awarded at the End of Year School Assembly.

 

All essays submitted to 华文创想曲 (see last week’s email for more info on 华文创想曲) will be automatically entered into our own Essay Contest.
