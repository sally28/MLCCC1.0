Dear Parents,

 

As the Coronavirus threat in Wuhan and China continues to improve, 

We now face an uphill battle in the USA, the country we live in and call home. 

Penn Medicine has been leading the way in this battle and the frontline medical staff are facing a critical shortage of protective medical PPE supplies such as N95 masks. They need our help! They need our help badly!

Penn Medicine has been very supportive of MLCCC in the past (with 4 grants in the last 5 years and being a generous Gala sponsor several years in a row). 

Now it is our turn to show that we care too as a community!

 

We are still collecting donations in support of Penn Medicine's frontline fight against the coronavirus. 

Please join us, make a donation and make a real difference as we face this pandemic together.

Please also ask if your company matches your donations, it makes a big difference!

A. Call for Blood Donations: CDC and American Red Cross Emergency Blood Drive Due to Coronavirus Outbreak

Donate today, you can save a life in 15 min! 

Critical Need For Blood and Platelets, Healthy donors urged to make an appointment now at: https://www.redcrossblood.org/donate-blood/dlp/coronavirus--covid-19--and-blood-donation.html?from=timeline&isappinstalled=0

 

Right now, the American Red Cross has an ongoing critical need for blood product donations as uncertainties remain during this coronavirus pandemic. Blood drives continue to be canceled at an alarming rate and patients need a sufficient blood supply throughout the many weeks of this crisis and beyond. Healthy individuals are needed to schedule an appointment to give in the days and weeks ahead to help patients counting on lifesaving blood, platelets or AB Elite plasma.

 

For more from the CDC on the urgent need for blood collection, watch this video: https://vimeo.com/397519259

B. Penn Medicine Healthcare Career Exploration for High School Students

Do you have a child in high school that is interested in the healthcare field? Penn Medicine has partnered with the Cradle of Liberty Council of Scouts to launch an Explorers Post. Meetings are for local high school students to learn from Penn Medicine employees about different ways they can pursue a career in healthcare beyond the notion that a student can only be a doctor or nurse. Through this partnership, the Penn Medicine Explorers Post hosts career exploration meetings once a month, highlighting different career opportunities in healthcare.

Meeting Schedule:

Our monthly meetings occur the last Tuesday of each month from 5:00pm-6:30pm on the Penn Presbyterian Medical Center (PPMC) Campus in the Philadelphia Heart Institute (PHI) Auditorium. Below is our schedule for the rest of the school year.

February – Information Services/Information Technology

March – Nursing

April – Medical Simulation Education (tentative)

May – Paramedic/EMT/PennSTAR (tentative)
