MLCCC School Newsletter #20210509

Dear Parents,

Except for a few classes, this Sunday is the last day of our school year. It is hard to believe that we finished the year virtually, again!

Together, we have weathered through an unsettling year of Covid-19 and social instability. Regardless of what is ahead of us, we are both grateful and hopeful. We would like to wish the MLCCC community a very Happy Mother’s Day; and all of our teachers a fantastic Teacher Appreciation Week (May  3 to May 7). Thank you to all the moms for taking care of things. Thank you to our entire teaching staff and their families for their dedication. Thank you to PTO, Club 166, and everyone who has supported us through this year of turbulence. Let us enjoy our freer Sundays, though we hope to see everyone in our online summer camp as we continue to  learn and fight against the Covid-19 educational slide! :)

4. First Issue of “MLCCC Monthly Check-Ins” Magazine

We are proud to announce that the MLCCC Junior Reporter Class will release its first issue of the “MLCCC Monthly Check-Ins” magazine this week.

Under the guidance of teacher Ms. Xu, our students worked hard to put together this inaugural issue. The purpose of this magazine is to provide yet another platform for learning and applying Mandarin, including generating ideas, acquiring content, and introducing students to basic publishing techniques such as layout, template, design, and placement.

Together with MLCCC Podcast, Vlog, the Check-Ins magazine will bring Mandarin learning at MLCCC to another level. We are proud of the class’s efforts and success, and encourage more students to join in as contributing journalists.  学以致用， 领会贯通！（Practice makes perfect!)

大家都还记得明朗中文学校小记者班的同学们吗？

他们在明朗网络春晚的节目当中一展风采，以优越的表现和完美自信， 圆满完成了小小主持人的任务， 赢得了大家的好评。现在他们要学中文, 用中文再上一层楼，在徐老师的爱心，精心指导和家长们的支持下，创办了自己的中文月刊：“明朗每月消息” (MLCCC Monthly Check-Ins). Read the Issue #1HERE.

他们年龄小，可是志气大，勇敢接受挑战，为我们树立了学中文的好榜样！我们预祝他们首发成功，也希望大家给他们以最大的关注和支持。更希望更多的同学们也加入这个表现中文学习和助长个性与自信的崭新的学习平台。美好童年记忆从这里开始！

5. Podcast #17 - #20 on Children’s Book on Chinese American Nuclear Physicist

Our Podcast Team celebrated May’s Asian American and Pacific Islander Heritage Month by producing four special episodes on the Chinese-American nuclear physicist Dr. Chien-Shiung Wu, who was honored with a U.S. postage stamp issued this past January. Episodes 17-20 are centered around a children’s book “Queen of Physics” on Dr. Wu, authored by Ms. Teresa Robeson.  

Episode 17: dialogue in English with Ms. Teresa Robeson about her book “Queen of Physics”  

Episode 18: five of our students read Ms. Robeson’s book in Chinese

Episode 19: one of our second grade students and her mom read Ms. Robeson’s book in English

Episode 20: biography of Dr. Wu, read in Chinese and English by our students

What a terrific way to celebrate AAPI Heritage Month!  Thank you to the many people who made these four episodes possible, including but not limited to:  

Teresa Robeson, “Queen of Physics” author 作者

6. 2019-2020 School Year Awards

For the prior school year of 2019-2020, we were not able to hold our end-of-school-year Assembly to hand out awards (trophies and medals) because of Covid. Initially, we wanted to wait past Covid to still present the awards in person. But Covid has lasted so long that we decided to give out electronic awards. Additionally, for the Essay Contest winners, we had originally wanted to create a nice book of the essays as a memento for our young writers, but our usual printer is out of the country due to Covid and other printers having technical or quality issues, so the book cannot be done, unfortunately. For those students who submitted drawings for the cover of the Book of Essays last school year, please contact us.  We have a few drawings without names that we would like to identify the artist for.

We have started preparing the electronic award (Amazon e-gift cards) for each recipient. Thank you for your patience.  We will send a separate email recapping all the recipients.        

7. Step Up, Speak Up: MLCCC Student Yee-Yee LI Spoke at Stop Hate Rally on May 2

Yee-Yee Li is a 10-year-old student who attends the Baldwin School and our Weekend School.  Encouraged by her parents, and disheartened by the recent wave of racial violence toward Asian Americans, she wrote her own speech and spoke bravely at a rally at Philadelphia’s City Hall early last Sunday. Also at the rally were PA’s councilman and senator, and another student, Ian Sun of Radnor High School who also spoke up against racial injustice. They set a great example of citizenship and participation.  We are proud of them.  

Read their stories here: https://mlccc-community.blogspot.com/2021/05/stand-up-speak-up.html

If you feel strongly about this issue, you can write your own essay and we will interview you and share your article with the community.

Principal Xiong also joined this event, spoke at length at the Rally, and was interviewed by Beccah Hendrickson of 6ABC Philadelphia. 

https://6abc.com/society/activists-rally-across-tri-state-area-to-stop-asian-hate/10569726/

8. Persistency Award - COVID-Specific Adjustment

Although only our 2-hour Chinese language classes have been traditionally eligible to accrue toward this award, we are making an exception for our 1-hr virtual Kindergarten (CHL-K or Montessori K) classes this school year, as these classes were two hours in-person but we shortened to 1-hr over ZOOM to reduce screen time for our Kindergarten students.  In other words, the set of classes that were eligible to accrue toward this award before the pandemic remains the same and the award amounts remain the same.

2. In Celebration of the AAPI Heritage Month - Recommended Books for Both Children and Adults

This is a simple but carefully selected reading list, including books for all ages that highlight stories and experiences beyond the stereotypical depiction of the Asian American experience. Originally posted in Mandarin, the list went viral and now the article and the reading list have been translated into English, and are an incredible resource to people of all races who want to understand what it means to be Asian in America today.

The book list is divided into two parts: one book list for children, and one for adults.

Click here for the complete listing and detailed description of each book and its author.

3. May is Mental Health Awareness Month

May is Mental Health Awareness Month. Each May, individuals, and organizations around the U.S. come together to initiate conversations that raise awareness around mental health. The National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) has adopted a "You Are Not Alone" theme, focusing on the healing value of connection in striving to create a society where no one feels alone in their struggle. Mental Health America honors Mental Health Awareness Month with "Tools 2 Thrive", a campaign providing practical tools to improve mental health and increase resiliency.

MLCCC recommends multiple programs during May including live webinars from Minding Your Mind and UCA. Please check out the offerings listed below and join us for a virtual or pre-recorded presentation.
