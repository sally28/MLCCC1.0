1. Win Prizes During Our Gala By Answering Questions 明朗网络春晚有奖竞猜

Eligible Players: students/families currently enrolled in our Weekend School.

When: during our Virtual Gala YouTube Live Stream on Feb. 14, 7PM through past 9PM probably.

How Questions are Posed: announced by our masters of ceremony throughout the Virtual Gala.

How to Answer: 

- Via the Chat function of the YouTube Live Stream.  You need a Gmail email address or account to create a YouTube Channel (a few clicks) to enable the Chat function.  

- MUST type your name and class (Chinese class 中文班 if you have one, or non-language class 兴趣班 if you don’t take Chinese) with each answer that you give to be considered a Qualified answer.  

- Answers can be in English or Chinese.    

What the Questions are Like: 

- Will be asked in both Chinese and English.  

- Some can be general cultural knowledge while others are based on the performances in our Gala.  

Prizes: 

- Prizes begin at $5 Amazon gift card and go up to $50, $100 Gift Package or Gift Card from our sponsors.  

- School has final say in choosing the winners whose answer was Correct, Eligible, and Qualified.

- The more you answer correctly, the bigger your prize will be.  

- School will calculate the winners after our Gala and contact them.

   

More instructions on how to sign up for the Chat function, and more details will be shared when we are a little closer to Sunday.

UCA (United Chinese Americans) Youth Mental Health Awareness Campaign

MLCCC and UCA will join hands in promoting Youth Mental Health Awareness

Due to a unique set of familial, cultural, and societal factors, mental health issues among Asian Americans youth are on the rise but have received little attention. Working with local community organizations and a team of experts and counselors, UCA addresses this urgent need by creating culturally-informed programs to raise awareness and provide knowledge of mental health concerns. UCA has successfully organized 12 national and local youth mental health conferences, as well as two webinars specifically designed to address mental health needs resulting from the pandemic. We will continue to share more information in special mental health webinars with our community.

Let us include mental health in our daily conversation with our children, especially our teens and youth. It takes a community to raise meantally healthy children.

This campaign is in part supported by a generous grant from Penn Medicine's CARE Program (awarded to our Principal Mr. Qunbin Xiong in 2020).

Chien-Shiung Wu was a Chinese-American experimental physicist who made significant contributions in the field of nuclear physics. Wu worked on the Manhattan Project, where she helped develop the process for separating uranium into uranium-235 and uranium-238 isotopes by gaseous diffusion. -Wikipedia
