MLCCC School Newsletter #20210328

Dear Parents,

At MLCCC, we stand against racism, xenophobia, and acts of violence against any group of people for simply being who they are. We strongly condemn racial injustice in any shape or form!

Asian Americans across the United States have been on the move, taking to the streets, joining ZOOM webinars, using social media, sending emails and letters to politicians to strongly protest against the cowardly acts of violence. You can be a part of the movement too.

We want to bring parent’s attention to the psychological impact of racial attacks on our children:

According to Minding Your Mind, the resulting trauma from the reality of discrimination over generations will and has significantly impacted the emotional wellbeing and mental health of the Asian Americans community, especially our children. MLCCC will commit to being part of the solution (such as our mental wellness class). Thank you to local and national organizations such UCA and Minding Your Mind for providing and sharing resources with our community; feel free to reach out if you need help and report any incident that you and your loved ones have encountered to the organizations listed in the flyers attached.

   

We continue to call parent’s attention to the recent spikes in anti-Asian American attacks; please be alert and cautious in the public, especially for our seniors and children. Attached is some information regarding steps you can take and talks you can have with your children. (Parent Guide in Chinese. Parent Guide in English, COVID - Racism in Chinese, COVID- Racism in English)

 

We also encourage parents to participate in nationally organized social/digital campaigns for racial justice and equality. Also, our current Mental Health Wellness Class may help you if you want to have a conversation with your child(ren) about this topic. Feel free to let us know if you have any questions or need help (in...@mlccc.org).

听众朋友们， 大家好。 欢迎来到我们明朗中文播客第14集。 今天是2021年3月26日。 我是伍子颜， 这期明朗中文播客的主持人。今天我们要献给所有大家的是ADA LI 同学朗诵 的故事《知母草》。ADA LI 同学是CHARLESTOWN 小学四年级的学生，她兴趣广泛，平常最喜欢钢琴和花样游泳，还喜欢滑冰，滑雪，芭蕾和尤可里里等。

Ada 讲的真好。这个故事告诉了我们几个道理： 做好事不应该为了私心， 前面两个帮助老婆婆的人都是为了自己的好处， 只有樵夫是真心对待老婆婆， 没有别的目的。  另外， 只有像樵夫一样心肠好的人才会好好用老婆婆交给的本领，帮助更多的人，而不是为了自己赚钱。

4. The Ai Ling Guan Memorial Scholarship Fund - 2021 Application Opens, Due 5/1/2021

Scholarship Description:

Ms. Ai Ling Guan has taught Chinese for over 30 years in several countries. To commemorate her passion for teaching the Chinese language to both children and adults, her family has set up a scholarship fund to honor those teachers and students engaged in the study of the Chinese language in the US and Canada.

Visit https://wuchuanren.org/ to download proper application forms. We encourage all students and teachers to apply for this honorable award and recognition.

Recommendation Letter can be filled out in the form on their website or emailed to gal.sch...@gmail.com

Deadline: May-1-2021

Eligibility:

Students: Students aged 12 – 18 on or before May 1st of the year, studying Chinese at school, weekend /evening Chinese school, or via private tutoring

Teachers: Teachers of the Chinese language or Chinese school administrators

Residing in the US or Canada

The applicant has not previously won the award/scholarship

Awards:

Students: 14 winners, $500 each

Teachers: 3 winners, $1,000 each

Application Process:

Application open online: March 1st

Application due: May 1st

Results announcement: June 1st

Students: Scholarship will be paid directly to the Chinese school/tutor where the student studies Chinese and will serve as tuition. If she/he studies Chinese primarily at a public or private school, or if she/he does not plan to continue studying Chinese, the scholarship can be forwarded to fund other studies

Teachers: Award will be given directly to the winner in the form of a check.

3. Community Reading Recommendation by the Chinese American Civic Association

This week’s reading is: 美华史记 |21世纪的华人维权之路——陈建生案, selected work by the Chinese American Civic Association (CACA) and Historical Society of the Chinese Americans.

CACA’s mission is to empower the Chinese American community through education, leadership development, active participation in civic and public affairs, and charity and community improvement programs.

This summer, the Editor-in-Chief, Dr. Cathy Huang, Ph.D., will take our children back in history to travel the roads our early ancestor immigrants have traveled to the United States, to learn about their lives, their struggles, and their unique contribution to America, a country they liked to call home.
