2. Intermediate Drawing Camp Class 彩铅

This class is taught by Mr. Zhang Benbiao whose students have won awards. Class size is also small (8 students) to maximize learning. Class is taught mainly in Mandarin.

彩铅1 Tuesdays & Thursdays 1:00pm - 2:00pm EDT.  

彩铅2 Mondays & Wednesdays 1:00pm - 2:00pm EDT

Drawings done in the two classes are different but techniques taught are similar.

Attached at the bottom of this newsletter are samples of Mr. Zhang’s students’ artwork.

本课程从彩铅和素描基础训练入手，通过彩色铅笔的排线、平涂、渐变、叠色等技巧练习，掌握写实绘画的基本能力。本课程将素描和彩铅，静物写生与卡通画相融合，提高内容的创意性和学习的趣味性。也希望通过本活动能形成耐心、细致的学习习惯和培养良好的艺术情趣。

This course starts with learning the basics of drawing and sketching using colored pencils, and progresses to mastering the basics of realistic drawing through the practice of colored pencil techniques of lines, gradation, color layering and more, so as to improve their abilities to draw realistically. This class integrates still life sketching and cartoon drawing to improve the creativity of the content and the fun of learning. Through this class, patience and meticulous study habits can also be formed and artistic taste can also be cultivated.

3. First Issue of “MLCCC Monthly Check-Ins” Magazine

We are proud to announce that the MLCCC Junior Reporter Class will release its first issue of the “MLCCC Monthly Check-Ins” magazine this week.

Under the guidance of teacher Ms. Xu, our students worked hard to put together this inaugural issue. The purpose of this magazine is to provide yet another platform for learning and applying Mandarin, including generating ideas, acquiring content, and introducing students to basic publishing techniques such as layout, template, design, and placement.

Together with MLCCC Podcast, Vlog, the Check-Ins magazine will bring Mandarin learning at MLCCC to another level. We are proud of the class’s efforts and success, and encourage more students to join in as contributing journalists.  学以致用， 领会贯通！（Practice makes perfect!)

5月16日 星期天 西部时间5点，欢聚一堂听黄西侃！

现在就点击这里注册

5点(美西), 6点(山地), 7点(中部), 8点(美东)

针对亚裔种族歧视是不分你我他的。

出了名的华裔就不受歧视了吗？ 土生土长的华裔二代， 三代就不受歧视了吗？答案是：不！！

黄西去年的生日礼物：“Chink”

黄西为人和善，谦虚低调。去年他生日那天在洛杉矶演出，下台后听见一位喜剧演员用“Chink”这一种族歧视性的语言博得听众笑声，黄西毫不犹豫地冲到台上制止。类似的事情发生了好几次，黄西是如何让对方演员道歉的呢？

黄西在这本书里做了详细记载。

黄西选择在美国针对亚裔暴力时刻写出他的经历和看法，是为了帮助美国华裔认清种族歧视根源。黄西认为不能姑息种族歧视的语言，同时他提出了对付种族歧视的具体建议。

美国华人中63%是第一代移民。很多家长自己都不知道种族歧视的根源和如何应对，更没有办法教育自己的孩子。这不利于华裔孩子的族裔自信。所以黄西的书来得非常及时。

5月16日，带着孩子来见黄西，欢迎现场给黄西提问！

书里，一个你不知道的黄西

我们只认识那个台上穿着华丽服装，聚光下的黄西，那个被欢笑和赞扬声淹没了的黄西。疫情期间黄西终于能安静下来写本书。这本书告诉了一个你从不知道的黄西，原来一个真正的黄西远远大于喜剧演员黄西。

他在书里谈到：迄今为止他18年的喜剧演员漫漫路经历了哪些波折，收获了哪些喜悦。无论是独自驾驶奔赴人烟稀少的地区演出，还是站在拜登对面，这位博士级别，第一代移民出身的喜剧演员都展示出他对事业，家庭和的执着。

书中还透露了他曾经陷入抑郁求医，他因异地演出远离妻儿的惆怅，老父母签证数次被拒绝的遗憾。

然而最令人肃然起敬的是黄西的高尚品格和维护族裔尊严的意志。喜剧演员职业本身前景就充满不确定性，而作为一个移民出身的黄西很清楚，任何机会的出现都可能立即消失再也不会回来。某天终于一个可以出名，收获高报酬的机会找到了他，因为这个角色有损华人形象，黄西拒绝了！

2. In Celebration of the AAPI Heritage Month - Recommended Books for Both Children and Adults

3. May is Mental Health Awareness Month

May is Mental Health Awareness Month. Each May, individuals, and organizations around the U.S. come together to initiate conversations that raise awareness around mental health. The National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) has adopted a "You Are Not Alone" theme, focusing on the healing value of connection in striving to create a society where no one feels alone in their struggle. Mental Health America honors Mental Health Awareness Month with "Tools 2 Thrive", a campaign providing practical tools to improve mental health and increase resiliency.

MLCCC recommends multiple programs during May including live webinars from Minding Your Mind and UCA. Please check out the offerings listed below and join us for a virtual or pre-recorded presentation.
