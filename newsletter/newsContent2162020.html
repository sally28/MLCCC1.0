 1. School Resumes This Sunday, February 16th, 2020

 After the cancellation of our 2020 New Year gala, and two weeks closing due to the new virus concerns, we will resume our weekend school this Sunday, 02/16/2020. Thanks for your patience and welcome back!

 

2. Updated Community-Wide Virus Concerns

As of today (02/13/20202), we are not aware of anyone in our community, and the tri-state area, for that matter, that has any (confirmed or unconfirmed) symptoms of the Coronavirus. Jan 26’s Gala and Feb 2’s and 9th’s school were canceled because information on the Coronavirus was just starting to appear then and information was very limited, plus there were limited response and guidance from any U.S. departments, plus the Gala’s Show is held in an enclosed room with hundreds of people, including especially young children and elderly. With timely updates from CDC and other sources, At this time, the CDC and the US government have taken very strong measures to contain any suspicious cases and closely monitor a few cases far & wide who are tested positive, the situation is well under control, CDC has stated that the US general public’s risk of infection is very LOW. 

Under those circumstances, we feel confident and safe enough to resume our school this Sunday to continue our children’s learning.

 

3. Cautionary Steps the School Asks of our Entire Community this Sunday

Remember, coronavirus is a type of virus, just like a flu virus, so the same personal and public hygienes also apply to the prevention of this new virus, we ask all to observe and abide by the following recommendations and rules:

  1. Those who traveled from China within the last 24 days, please observe a strict self-isolation at home and do NOT come to school.  

  2. Anyone who has been in close contact with people in (1), please also observe a self-isolate at home and do NOT come to school.  

  3. Anyone who has cold, flu, or respiratory illness symptoms (fever, cough, shortness of breath, etc.), please seek medical help right away and do NOT come to school.

  4. If during school hours we discover anyone in (1), (2), or (3) above, we ask you to please go home to start a self-isolation and not return to school until you are cleared medically.

  5. The school reserves the right to enforce these rules.

 

Campus-Wide Precautions and Instructions:

  1. We will have hand sanitizer stations by the main entrance so that everyone who comes in must first get hand sanitizer.

  2. Parents, please pack hand sanitizer and tissues for each of your children so that there are plenty of hand sanitizers and tissues in each classroom.

  3. School Store, Snack and beverage stations such as bubble tea, Tea Club, and School Store will be suspended until further notice.

  4. We ask everyone to cover coughs and sneezes with their elbows or into tissues (not with their hands), to refrain from shaking hands or touching parts of the face, and to wash their hands with soap frequently for 20+ seconds each wash. According to some research, washing your hands thoroughly is the most effective way to stop viruses/germs spread.

  5. This is also a great opportunity to teach our children the correct personal hygiene and to learn about germs/viruses preventions.

We have attached some graphics and other instructions to practice the recommended hygiene and prevention practices. More reading on personal hygiene and prevention.

 

4. Updated info on the coronavirus from CDC

CDC most up-to-date Coronavirus situation summary can be found here:

https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-nCoV/summary.html

Briefly, Risk Assessment on coronavirus from CDC (as of 02/13/2020):

Outbreaks of novel virus infections among people are always of public health concern. The risk from these outbreaks depends on the characteristics of the virus, including how well it spreads between people, the severity of resulting illness, and the medical or other measures available to control the impact of the virus (for example, vaccine or treatment medications).

The potential public health threat posed by the 2019-nCoV virus is high, both globally and to the United States. The fact that this virus has caused illness, including illness resulting in death, and sustained person-to-person spread in China is concerning. These factors meet two of the criteria of a pandemic. It’s unclear how the situation will unfold, but the risk is dependent on exposure. At this time, some people will have an increased risk of infection, for example, healthcare workers caring for 2019-nCoV patients and other close contacts of 2019-to patients. For the general American public, who are unlikely to be exposed to this virus, the immediate health risk from 2019-nCoV is considered LOW at this time.
