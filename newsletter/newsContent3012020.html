2. Speech Contest on 03/15/2020 - Sign Up by This Sunday

In response to parents’ suggestions, no video upload this year. The contest will take place at school. Participating students are pulled from class to deliver their speech in front of judges and then returned to class. Please sign up for the contest at this link, by this Sunday.

 

- Format: Recite from memory in front of judges.

- Topic: Student's choice.

- Eligibility: Weekend School Grades 1 and up, and CSL Levels 1 and up.   

- Groups: Participants are grouped by grades. If participants are few, several grades can be combined to form one group. CSL is in a different group than CHL. If a student is currently not taking a Chinese class, the school determines which group to place students in.    

- Upload a copy of speech: we will publish the speeches in MLCCC's Official WeChat Platform and on ISSUU. Please upload a copy (MS Word, PDF, or Text file format) in the sign-up link above.       

- Scoring: based on pronunciation, volume and tone, fluency, presentation, and content.  See sign-up for scoring details. Please keep speeches between 1-3 minutes. Being shorter or longer causes point deductions.   

- If anyone wants to be a judge, please let us know.

6. School Store Opens But Other Cautionary Steps Continue This Sunday

  1. Those who traveled from China within the last 24 days, please observe a strict self-isolation at home and do NOT come to school.  

  2. Anyone who has been in close contact with people in (1), please also observe a self-isolate at home and do NOT come to school.  

  3. Anyone who has cold, flu, or respiratory illness symptoms (fever, cough, shortness of breath, etc.), please seek medical help right away and do NOT come to school.

  4. If during school hours we discover anyone in (1), (2), or (3) above, we ask you to please go home to start a self-isolation and not return to school until you are cleared medically.

  5. The school reserves the right to enforce these rules.

 

Campus-Wide Precautions Continue But School Store Opens:

  1. We will have hand sanitizer stations by the main entrance so that everyone who comes in must first get hand sanitizer.

  2. Parents, please pack hand sanitizer and tissues for each of your children so that there are plenty of hand sanitizers and tissues in each classroom.

  3. We ask everyone to cover coughs and sneezes with their elbows or into tissues (not with their hands), to refrain from shaking hands or touching parts of the face, and to wash their hands with soap frequently for 20+ seconds each wash. According to some research, washing your hands thoroughly is the most effective way to stop viruses/germs spread.

  4. This is also a great opportunity to teach our children the correct personal hygiene and to learn about germs/viruses preventions.

  5.  Thoroughly washing hands with soap and running water is the most effective way to prevent virus spread! 

 

We have attached some graphics and other instructions on the recommended hygiene practices. More reading on personal hygiene and prevention.


We are also closely following the CDC and the PA/NJ/DE State's Health Departments for the updates on Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID-19). CDC has the most updated information as it becomes available. The recent CDC Situation Summary can be found here: https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/summary.html.

A. Call for Donations in Support of People of Wuhan: Make a difference together!

In association with ABD International, Inc. a longtime MLCCC partner, we are calling for donations to support the people of Wuhan in their heroic fight against the new coronavirus. 

Recently, we have received inquiries from parents and friends if they can donate money to support the people of Wuhan on their frontline battle against the virus. After a discussion with our community partner ABD International, we decided to collect ONLY monetary donations due to the manpower and time involved in sending materials to the frontline. 

We have worked out with the Chinese Language and Culture Education Foundation of China 中国华文教育基金会 to forward our donations to the most needed hospitals in Wuhan. We will also publicize the details of donations and the recipient information to the community. Check out our “Stay Strong Wuhan! Stay Strong China!” video. 

 

You can donate in multiple ways, including No-Fee-donations online!

- Online donation link; (Online accepting Paypal and all major credit cards)

- Personal check (payable to “MLCCC”, include a note “In support of Wuhan”); 

- Cash;

- Company match. If your company has a match program, please take advantage of it.

 

 

B. Penn Medicine Healthcare Career Exploration for High School Students

Do you have a child in high school that is interested in the healthcare field? Penn Medicine has partnered with the Cradle of Liberty Council of Scouts to launch an Explorers Post. Meetings are for local high school students to learn from Penn Medicine employees about different ways they can pursue a career in healthcare beyond the notion that a student can only be a doctor or nurse. Through this partnership, the Penn Medicine Explorers Post hosts career exploration meetings once a month, highlighting different career opportunities in healthcare.

Meeting Schedule:

Our monthly meetings occur the last Tuesday of each month from 5:00pm-6:30pm on the Penn Presbyterian Medical Center (PPMC) Campus in the Philadelphia Heart Institute (PHI) Auditorium. Below is our schedule for the rest of the school year.

February – Information Services/Information Technology

March – Nursing

April – Medical Simulation Education (tentative)

May – Paramedic/EMT/PennSTAR (tentative)
