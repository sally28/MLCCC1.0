Math Enrichment for grades 3-4, 5-6 in September  

July 8 - July 20, taught by Ms. Tan Xianfang.

Course content and teacher are different from last summer.  

Grades 3-4 topics include:

* Properties of addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division

* Fractions

* Decimals

* Real-world problems such as length, weight, time, etc.

* Basic geometry such as angles, perimeter, area, volume, etc.

Grades 5-6 topics include:

* Arithmetic: addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, power/exponents

* Number Theory such as multiples, factors, prime and prime factorization, etc.

* Fractions

* Decimals

* Equations and inequalities

* Percentage, ratio, and proportions

* Basic geometry (angles, perimeter, area, volume, etc.)

* Statistics (basic statistics, counting, probability, etc.)

Dr. Tan is a teacher who mainly teaches college-level physics and electrical engineering courses. In the past few years, she also dedicated herself to teaching math enrichment courses of elementary/middle school levels at Chinese Schools during weekends/summer.

 

NEW: Pre-MATHCOUNTS and MATHCOUNTS for Grades 5-8 in September

July 22 - August 2, taught by Mr. Tom Le (Radnor School District official MATHCOUNTS coach, MLCCC math teacher)

Improve math techniques using MATHCOUNTS questions and critical thinking.

Mr. Le has been teaching at MLCCC Weekend School and Summer Camp for many years and is one of our students' favorite teachers.  His techniques are refreshing, effective, and connects well with the students.

Regarding MATHCOUNTS, many students in our area who have advanced from the County Level to the State Level competition were once his students, including 2016's Pennsylvania MATHCOUNTS 1st Place Individual who advanced to the National Competition.  Both of his children have also advanced to the State Level multiple times, including this year.

 

English Writing for Grades 3-6 in September

July 8 - July 20, taught by Ms. Francine Lee-Kim (PA certified K-12 teacher)

Course content is different from last summer.  

Throughout the two weeks, students will learn about and participate in all elements of the writing workshop process from brainstorming to editing, and sharing and receiving ideas and refining their work.  They will read mentor texts to study and imitate in their own writing. They will be writing for various text types and purposes. Genres such as opinion, explanatory, research, and narrative writing will be integrated into fun ways to keep students engaged.  Grammar and mechanics will also be studied to improve the readability of their pieces.

Fencing for ages 7-14, one week only

July 29 - August 2, taught by Mr. Graham Draper (YMCA coach, MLCCC fencing coach)

Fencing, known as “physical chess”, is a graceful and fast sport that hones mental agility.  The class will emphasize having fun while practicing discipline, acquiring strength and improving gross motor skills, using a variety of games to promote strength, coordination, and tactics.
